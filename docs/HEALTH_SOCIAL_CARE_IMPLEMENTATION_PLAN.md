# Health and Social Care / Health and Social Studies — Full Implementation Plan

**Purpose:** Make learning effective and engaging so students cover all test topics and build the knowledge, application, investigation, and evaluation skills required by GCSE Health and Social Care assessment objectives.  
**Specification:** Pearson Edexcel GCSE Health and Social Care (2HS01 single award, 2HS02 double award). *Note: AQA does not offer Health and Social Care GCSE; Pearson Edexcel and WJEC do. This plan uses Edexcel as the primary reference.*  
**Date:** February 2025.

---

## 1. Exam Board and Topic List (Summary)

### 1.1 Qualification Structure

| Award | Units | External | Internal (controlled assessment) |
|-------|-------|----------|----------------------------------|
| **Single Award** | Units 1 + 2 | Unit 1 (40%) | Unit 2 (60%) |
| **Double Award** | Units 1–4 | Unit 1 (20%) + Unit 4 (20%) | Unit 2 (30%) + Unit 3 (30%) |

**Assessment objectives (approx.):** AO1 Recall, select and communicate knowledge (25–35%); AO2 Apply skills, plan and carry out investigations (40–50%); AO3 Analyse and evaluate, make reasoned judgements (20–30%). Quality of written communication assessed in all units.

### 1.2 Unit Overview

| Unit | Title | Assessment | Weight (Single) | Weight (Double) |
|------|-------|------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Unit 1** | Understanding Personal Development and Relationships | External exam (1h 15m, 70 marks) | 40% | 20% |
| **Unit 2** | Exploring Health, Social Care and Early Years Provision | Internal task (50 marks) | 60% | 30% |
| **Unit 3** | Promoting Health and Wellbeing | Internal task (50 marks) | — | 30% |
| **Unit 4** | Health, Social Care and Early Years in Practice | External exam (1h 15m, 70 marks) | — | 20% |

**Unit 1 exam format:** 15 multiple-choice questions + case study/scenario questions (short–medium answers).  
**Unit 4 exam format:** Three compulsory questions based on case studies and short scenarios.

## 2. Full AQA / Edexcel Topic List (By Unit)

### 2.1 Unit 1 — Understanding Personal Development and Relationships

#### Topic 1.1 Human growth and development

- **Life stages:** infancy (0–2), early childhood (3–8), adolescence (9–18), early adulthood (19–45), middle adulthood (46–65), later adulthood (65+).
- **Physical growth and development:** gross and fine motor skills across life stages.
- **Intellectual/cognitive development:** language development across life stages.
- **Emotional maturity:** bonding and attachment; self-image, self-esteem and self-concept.
- **Self-concept:** how it develops across life stages; affected by gender, appearance.
- **Social development:** formation of relationships; socialisation process.

#### Topic 1.2 Factors affecting human growth and development

- **Physical factors:** genetic inheritance, illness, disease, diet, exercise, alcohol, smoking.
- **Social, cultural and emotional factors:** family, friends, educational experiences, employment/unemployment, community involvement, religion, gender, ethnicity, sexual orientation, culture, relationship formation (marriage, divorce).
- **Economic factors:** income, wealth, employment status, occupation, social class, poverty, material possessions.
- **Physical environment:** pollution, noise, housing conditions, rural/urban lifestyles.
- **Psychological factors:** stress, relationships within family, friends, partners.
- **Link to self-concept:** how factors affect formation and change of self-concept; genetic vs environmental effects on growth and development.

#### Topic 1.3 Effects of relationships on personal growth and development

- **Types of relationships:** family (marriage, divorce, parenthood, siblings, blended families); friendships; intimate, personal and sexual relationships; working relationships.
- **Importance:** effect of these relationships on growth and development across all six life stages.

#### Topic 1.4 The effect of life events on personal development

- **Expected and unexpected life events:**
  - Relationship changes: marriage, living with partner, birth of children, death of partner/relatives/friend.
  - Physical changes: puberty, menopause, accidents and injury.
  - Life circumstances: starting school, further education, relocation (national/international), entering employment, promotion, retirement, redundancy, unemployment.
- **How they affect personal development:** lead to new learning; how to manage change.
- **Support networks:** partners, family, friends; professional carers and statutory services; community, voluntary and faith-based services.

### 2.2 Unit 2 — Exploring Health, Social Care and Early Years Provision

#### Topic 2.1 The range of care needs of major client groups

- **Client groups:** infants; children; adolescents; early adulthood; middle adulthood; later adulthood; individuals with specific needs.
- **Physical, intellectual, emotional and social needs:** hierarchy of needs; how failure to meet needs affects development.
- **How services respond:** universal vs targeted services; social policy goals; assessing population needs.
- **Why individuals access services:** temporary or permanent needs.

#### Topic 2.2 How health, social care and early years services are provided

- **Range of services** for each client group.
- **Types of providers:** statutory (NHS Trusts, integrated children’s services, extended services, local authority); private (private companies, self-employed practitioners, outsourcing); voluntary (charities, local support groups, non-profit); informal (family, friends, neighbours).
- **Organisation:** national and local; relationship between organisations; partnership and multi-agency working; integrated services (e.g. Sure Start, virtual wards, multi-agency disability teams, key workers, extended services).

#### Topic 2.3 How services are accessed and barriers to access

- **Ways to access:** self-referral; professional referral; third party referral.
- **Barriers:** physical (stairs, no lifts, lack of adaptations); psychological (stigma, fear of loss of independence); financial (means testing, charges, fees); geographical (distance, poor transport); cultural/language (beliefs, first language); resource (staff shortages, postcode lottery, lack of funding, demand).

#### Topic 2.4 Workers in health, social care and early years

- **Direct carers:** doctors, nurses, community nurses, health visitors, midwives, health care assistants, portage workers, child development workers, early years practitioners, family support workers, occupational therapists, physiotherapists, teachers.
- **Indirect carers:** practice managers, medical receptionists, school reception staff, catering staff.
- **Indirect services (outsourced):** cleaners, catering, security, portering, waste management.
- **Skills and qualifications** needed to deliver services effectively.

#### Topic 2.5 Care values which underpin service provider interaction

- **Care values:** promoting anti-discriminatory practice; promoting and supporting individual rights to dignity, independence, health and safety; promoting effective communication and relationships; maintaining confidentiality; acknowledging individual personal beliefs and identity.
- **Reflection in practice:** how care values are reflected in behaviour, attitudes and work of care practitioners.

#### Topic 3.1 Understanding health and wellbeing

- **Definitions of health and wellbeing:** different models and perspectives; holistic view.

#### Topic 3.2 Factors affecting health and wellbeing

- **Common factors:** lifestyle, environment, genetics, access to services, social support, employment, income, education.

- **For individuals or small groups:** planning and evaluating health improvement activities.

## 3. Design Principles for Effective & Engaging Learning

- **Align with specification:** Every activity should map to unit content and AO1/AO2/AO3. Quality of written communication must be practised.
- **Case study first:** Unit 1 and Unit 4 exams use case studies and short scenarios. All learning should use realistic scenarios and service user contexts.
- **Client group coverage:** Content must cover all major client groups (infants, children, adolescents, early/middle/later adulthood, individuals with specific needs) across health, early years, care of older people, and specific needs settings.
- **Investigation skills:** Unit 2 and Unit 3 are internally assessed. Students need practice planning investigations, gathering evidence, analysing and evaluating, and presenting conclusions.
- **Care values thread:** Anti-discriminatory practice, dignity, independence, communication, confidentiality, beliefs and identity should recur across all learning modes.
- **Life stages and development:** Strong emphasis on PIES (physical, intellectual, emotional, social) across all six life stages; factors affecting development; relationships and life events.
- **Reuse app patterns:** Mirror Science Lab / Maths Mastery / Business Hub / History Hub: concept → flashcard → check → questions; progress and gating; config-driven content.

## 5. Learning Modes (Effective & Engaging)

### 5.1 Concept Lab (Learn)

- **Purpose:** Build understanding before terminology and exam-style questions.
- **Content per concept:** One “core idea” (1–2 sentences); optional visual (e.g. life stages timeline, PIES diagram, hierarchy of needs, care value examples); “common misconception”; “apply to a scenario” (e.g. “How might a teenager’s self-concept be affected by social media?”).
- **Format:** Card or short page per concept; link to related topics and to flashcards/questions.
- **Coverage:** At least one concept per topic (1.1–4.4); more for dense topics (life stages, care values, barriers to access, factors affecting development).
- **Engagement:** Real-world scenarios; “What would a care worker do?” prompts; diversity and cultural sensitivity examples.

### 5.2 Key Terms & Flashcards

- **Purpose:** Secure AO1 (knowledge of key terms, life stages, care values, roles, barriers).
- **Content:** Term → definition; “in context” sentence; link to unit/topic. Include PIES, hierarchy of needs, self-concept, statutory/private/voluntary/informal, referral types, care values.
- **Modes:** Classic flip card; “type the term” or “match term–definition”; confidence rating for spaced repetition.
- **Coverage:** Full spec glossary (e.g. self-concept, gross motor skills, statutory provision, self-referral, anti-discriminatory practice, multi-agency working, etc.).
- **Engagement:** Group by unit and topic; “Exam tip” for terms that often appear in case study answers; “Care value” callouts.

### 5.3 Life Stages Timeline & PIES Map

- **Purpose:** Secure chronological understanding of development across six life stages; visualise PIES at each stage.
- **Content:** Interactive timeline; expandable cards per life stage with physical, intellectual, emotional, social milestones; “What develops when?” and “What factors affect this stage?” links.
- **Format:** Scrollable timeline; optional “match life stage to characteristic” and “order developmental milestones” drag-and-drop.
- **Coverage:** All six life stages with key PIES features; links to Factors (1.2) and Relationships (1.3).
- **Engagement:** Visual, age-appropriate; hook questions (“When do most people learn to walk?”).

### 5.4 Quick Check (Micro-Assessments)

- **Purpose:** Check recall and simple application before unlocking Case Study Lab and Question Lab.
- **Formats:**
  - Multiple choice (life stage, care value, barrier type, role, referral type).
  - True/False.
  - Short “one phrase” or “one term” (e.g. “What do we call care provided by family and friends?” → “Informal provision”).
  - Drag-and-drop: order life stages; match factor to type; match care value to example; match barrier to category.
  - “Which two of the following…” (AO1/AO2).
- **Coverage:** 5–10 items per topic.
- **Gating:** Unlock Case Study Lab and Question Lab per unit when Quick Check passed (e.g. ≥70% or “all correct once”).

### 5.5 Case Study Lab (Exam-Style Application)

- **Purpose:** Mirror Unit 1 and Unit 4: short scenarios and case studies with questions.
- **Content:** Case study (2–4 paragraphs) involving a service user in a setting (health, early years, older people, specific needs); 3–5 questions: define, describe, explain, analyse, evaluate. Mark-scheme-style breakdown: knowledge, application, analysis.
- **Coverage:**
  - Unit 1: Case studies covering life stages, factors, relationships, life events, self-concept.
  - Unit 4: Case studies covering care needs, care values, relationships, health promotion.
- **Engagement:** Variety of client groups and settings; “What care value applies here?” prompts; “Support network” analysis.

### 5.6 Investigation Lab (Internal Assessment Skills)

- **Purpose:** Support Unit 2 and Unit 3 controlled assessment: plan investigations, gather evidence, analyse, evaluate, present conclusions.
- **Content:** Guided investigation tasks: “Investigate the needs of one service user”; “Plan a health improvement activity for an individual or small group.” Structured support: aim, methods, evidence to gather, analysis prompts, evaluation prompts, conclusion template.
- **Format:** Step-by-step form or structured report template; model answers and mark-scheme breakdown (recall, plan and carry out, apply, analyse and evaluate).
- **Coverage:** 2–3 investigation scaffolds per unit (Unit 2 and Unit 3); scenarios from different client groups.
- **Engagement:** Realistic scenarios; “What would you ask?” and “What evidence would you look for?” prompts; link to care values and professional roles.

### 5.7 Care Values Practice

- **Purpose:** Practise identifying and applying care values in scenarios.
- **Content:** Short scenario + “Which care value(s) apply?”; “How would a care worker demonstrate [value]?”; “What would be wrong with [behaviour]?”
- **Format:** Card with scenario + structured short answer or multiple choice; model answer with value and example.
- **Coverage:** All five care values (anti-discriminatory, rights, communication, confidentiality, beliefs/identity) across units.
- **Engagement:** “Spot the breach” style; “How to put it right” follow-up.

### 5.8 Question Lab (Describe, Explain, Analyse, Evaluate)

- **Purpose:** Exam-style AO1+AO2+AO3 practice: short and medium-length answers; case study questions.
- **Content:**
  - **Describe:** “Describe two ways…” (e.g. two factors affecting development; two types of relationship).
  - **Explain:** “Explain why…” (e.g. why a service user might face barriers; why self-concept changes).
  - **Analyse:** “Analyse how…” (e.g. how life events affect development; how services work together).
  - **Evaluate:** “Evaluate the importance of…” or “To what extent…” (e.g. evaluate support networks; evaluate a health promotion method).
- **Format:** Structured form or free text with word limit; model answer and mark-scheme breakdown.
- **Coverage:** At least one of each question type per topic; mix of Unit 1 and Unit 4 style.
- **Engagement:** “Method mark” style feedback; QWC reminder.

### 5.9 Topic Overview & Revision Map

- **Purpose:** Show coverage at a glance; “your award” and “what’s next” per unit/topic.
- **Content:** Per unit: list of topics with status (Concept, Flashcards, Quick Check, Case Study, Investigation, Question Lab). Progress bars per unit. “Recommended next” (e.g. “Complete Quick Check for Unit 1 Topic 1.2 to unlock Case Study Lab”).
- **Engagement:** Award filter (Single vs Double); “Exam readiness” indicator per unit.

## 9. Engagement Tactics (Summary)

- **Scenarios and cases:** Every unit uses realistic service user scenarios (health, early years, older people, specific needs).
- **Client group variety:** Rotate infants, children, adolescents, adults, older people, individuals with specific needs so content feels broad and applicable.
- **Care values thread:** Recurring “Which care value?” and “How would a care worker…?” prompts.
- **Life stages visual:** Timeline and PIES map make development tangible and memorable.
- **Investigation skills:** Step-by-step scaffolds reduce anxiety about controlled assessment; model “plan, gather, analyse, evaluate, conclude.”
- **Real-world hooks:** Where useful, reference real services (NHS, Sure Start, local authority) within spec.
- **Clear path:** “Concept → Flashcards → Life Stages → Quick Check → Case Study / Investigation / Question Lab” visible in UI.
- **Progress and gating:** Unlock harder tasks after basics; show progress per unit.
- **Varied formats:** Mix MCQ, drag-and-drop, short text, structured investigation, case study analysis.
- **QWC reminder:** Quality of written communication callouts on extended answers.
